The Basics of Physics: A Beginner's Guide

Physics is the study of matter, energy, and the interactions between them. It forms the foundation for understanding how the universe works—from everyday objects to cosmic phenomena.

1. What is Physics?
Physics explains how and why objects move, how forces act on matter, and how energy is transferred.

2. Key Concepts
Force: A push or pull that changes motion.
Motion: Speed, direction, and acceleration.
Energy: The ability to do work.
Gravity: The attraction between masses.

3. Newton’s Laws of Motion
First Law: Objects remain at rest or in motion unless acted upon.
Second Law: Force equals mass times acceleration (F = ma).
Third Law: Every action has an equal and opposite reaction.

4. Why Physics Matters
Physics plays a crucial role in technology, transportation, medicine, and understanding natural phenomena.
